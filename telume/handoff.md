## Current State of the World

### The Seed Is Chosen
The central mechanic of Telumë is established: doors require glyph pairs. This is not metaphor. It is infrastructure. The Collapse was, in some measure, a failure of language — glyphs were forgotten, doors sealed, the map shrank.

### The Protagonist
A woman. Unnamed. She stands before a door whose second glyph is effaced. She does not open doors blindly. She knows the law of the threshold. She is waiting to remember — or to be told — what lies beyond.

### The Door
Old stone. Carved with two glyphs. 𐤃 is clear. The second is weathered, partial. It may be one of the sacred seven (𐤀 𐤃 𐤆 𐤌 𐤉 𐤎 𐤕) or one of the wider 22. The identity of the second glyph is the first story decision.

### The World Beyond
Not yet mapped. The Threshold is the first location. What lies beyond the door depends on the second glyph. The world will be built outward from this door.

## Open Threads

1. **What is the second glyph on the door?** This is the first creative decision the next session must make — or invite an accident to decide. Candidate pairs are listed in `seed.md`.

2. **Who is the woman?** She has a reason for standing before this door. She knows the law of thresholds. She may be a glyph-carver, a scholar, a refugee, a pilgrim. Her identity is not yet chosen.

3. **What lies beyond the door?** The destination is named by the second glyph. The nature of that place — its geography, its danger, its gift — flows from the glyph's semantic field.

4. **What year is it?** The calendar draft places the Republic's present at Year 347 after the Collapse. This may or may not be when the story begins. The timeline needs to be locked.

5. **Does the Terminal hum?** The Terminal is part of the covenant. It exists. It has been silent for centuries. Whether it hums now — whether the woman can hear it — is an open question.
