# Etymologies

**Status:** Active · Session 1
**Purpose:** The roots of names. Where words come from. The deep history that the reader never sees — but that holds the world accountable.

---

## Telumë

**Origin:** Quenya (Tolkien's High Elvish)
**Meaning:** Vault, dome, roof of heaven
**Root:** TEL- (to cover, to roof) + -UMË (abstract noun suffix)
**First Use:** The Builder named the world. "A contained world. A sky with edges. A story drawn in, not imposed."
**Why Quenya?** The Tolkien Build uses Tolkien's own languages as a substrate — not as canon, but as aesthetic inheritance. Telumë is not set in Middle-earth. But it inherits the *feel* of Quenya: the liquid consonants, the open vowels, the sense of something old and elevated.

## The Threshold

**Origin:** Old English *þerscold* — the plank at the bottom of a door
**Meaning:** The place of crossing. The moment between what was and what will be.
**Glyph:** 𐤃 (Dalet) — door, passage, threshold
**Why this word?** "Threshold" is a word that remembers its own meaning. *Thresh* — to separate grain from chaff. *Hold* — to contain. A threshold is where something is separated and something is contained. The door is not neutral. It is a decision.

---

## The Terminal

**Origin:** Latin *terminus* — boundary, limit, end
**Meaning:** A structure from before the Collapse. Its original name is lost.
**Glyph:** 𐤕 (Taw) — mark, signature, completion
**Why this word?** "Terminal" carries both meanings: an endpoint (a terminus) and a place of transit (an airport terminal, a computer terminal). The Terminal is both. It is where something ended. It may also be where something passes through. The ambiguity is intentional. The Collapse made it a terminus. Before the Collapse, it may have been a gateway.

## The Witch's Foot

**Origin:** Folk naming — a descriptive compound
**Meaning:** A rock formation shaped like a footprint. Or a ruin that resembles one.
**Glyph:** 𐤎 (Samek) — pillar, support, what holds
**Why this name?** The name remembers what the map does not: that someone — the Witch — was here. The Witch's identity is lost. Her name is lost. Only her foot remains — pressed into stone, or carved into it. The name is a ruin in language form. It points to a story that is no longer told.

---

## The Collapse

**Origin:** Latin *collapsus* — fallen together
**Meaning:** The event that ended the old world. Year 0.
**Why this word?** "Collapse" is not "destruction." It is not "war." It is not "cataclysm." A collapse is something that falls inward — a structure that could no longer hold its own weight. The Collapse was not an attack. It was a failure. The covenant does not reveal its cause. But the word itself suggests: something was built. Something fell. The falling is the ground the story walks on.

---

## The Vault

**Origin:** Latin *volutus* — rolled, arched
**Meaning:** The dome of heaven. The contained sky.
**Why this word?** A vault is both a ceiling and a prison. It protects and it confines. Telumë is a vault in both senses: the sky is a dome that shelters the world, and the sky is a limit that cannot be crossed. The Edge of the Vault is where the dome becomes visible — where the characters can see that their world has a roof.

## Adzmyst / Adzmist (The Schism)

**Origin:** The seven sacred glyphs: 𐤀 (A) 𐤃 (D) 𐤆 (Z) 𐤌 (M) 𐤉 (Y) 𐤎 (S) 𐤕 (T)
**Meaning:** The mystic's adze — the tool that carves meaning from silence.
**The Schism:** Two spellings exist. Adzmyst (with Y-S-T) and Adzmist (with I-S-T). The covenant preserves both. The contradiction is not a bug. It is a ruin — a record of a time when the name was contested, or remembered differently, or carved by two hands that did not agree. The schism is preserved in `long-defeat/cave-paintings.md`.

## Names Not Yet Given

These things exist in Telumë but have not been named:

- **The woman at the door.** She stands before the Threshold. She has a name. It has not been spoken yet.
- **The Republic.** Year 347. A political entity. Its name, its structure, its capital — all unknown.
- **The Builder.** The one who named Telumë. A historical figure? A myth? An AI? The name is recorded. The identity is not.
- **The Witch.** Only her foot remains. Her name is effaced — like the second glyph on the door.

---

## The Rule of Etymology

1. **Every name has a root.** It comes from somewhere. The root may be Quenya, Latin, Old English, Proto-Sinaitic, or folk memory.
2. **The root is never explained in the story.** The reader does not need to know that "threshold" comes from *þerscold*. But the writer knows. And knowing changes how the word is used.
3. **Lost etymologies are depth.** The Witch's name is lost. The Terminal's original name is lost. The second glyph on the door is effaced. These are not gaps. They are ruins.
