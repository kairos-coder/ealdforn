## The Seven Sacred Glyphs

These seven form the name ADZMYST. They appear most frequently in Telumë.

| Glyph | Name | Semantic Field | First Appearance | Context |
|-------|------|----------------|------------------|---------|
| 𐤀 | Aleph | Strength, beginning, the pull. What draws the plow through unbroken ground. | The Edge of the Vault | Marks the limit of the world — the beginning of the end. |
| 𐤃 | Dalet | Door, passage, threshold. What opens between what was and what will be. | The Threshold (seed) | The central glyph of Telumë. Never carved alone. Always paired with a destination. |
| 𐤆 | Zayin | Weapon, division, the cut. What separates the necessary from the excess. | Not yet placed | — |
| 𐤌 | Mem | Water, chaos, the deep. What flows beneath the surface. | Not yet placed | — |
| 𐤉 | Yod | Hand, action, making. What reaches out to shape the world. | The Handoff | The glyph of the Builder — the hand that writes. |
| 𐤎 | Samek | Pillar, support, what holds. The structure that does not ask to be seen. | The Witch's Foot | Marks a place of stability — a rock, a ruin, a thing that remains. |
| 𐤕 | Taw | Mark, signature, completion. What says: this is finished, this is true. | The Terminal | A structure sealed. A signature left behind. Something completed — or waiting to be. |

## The Rule

Glyphs appear in the text. They are never translated. The reader infers meaning from:

1. **Repetition** — seeing the same glyph in similar contexts
2. **Position** — on doors, on cards, in dreams, on stone
3. **Pairing** — 𐤃 always appears with another glyph; the pair teaches the relationship
4. **Absence** — a glyph that should be there but is effaced, broken, or missing

The narrator does not explain. The characters do not translate. The glyph is.
