# Phonemes of Telumë

**Status:** Sketch · Session 1
**Purpose:** To describe the sound of the world — not a full conlang, but an aesthetic guide for names, inscriptions, and the feel of spoken language.

---

## The Sound of the Vault

Telumë is not a language. It is a **place** — and places have sounds. The phonemes below describe what the world sounds like when names are spoken, when wind moves through stone, when a door groans open.

These sounds draw from:
- **Quenya** (Tolkien's High Elvish) — liquid, vowel-rich, melodic
- **Proto-Sinaitic** (Adzmyst) — consonantal roots, guttural depth
- **The feel of the vault** — contained, resonant, old

---

## Consonants

| Sound | Example | Feel |
|-------|---------|------|
| **L** | Telumë, Dalet | Liquid, flowing. The sound of water over stone. The sound of language itself. |
| **M** | Mem, Telumë | Closing. A door shutting. A mouth sealing. The hum of the Terminal. |
| **D** | Dalet, door | Threshold. The tongue against the teeth — the moment before speech. |
| **T** | Taw, Terminal | Finality. The stop. The mark at the end of the word. |
| **S** | Samek, Sibyl | Hissing. Wind through a crack. The whisper of something not quite heard. |
| **K/Q** | Quenya, Qoph | Deep. Guttural. The sound of stone on stone. The old tongue. |
| **TH** | Threshold | Soft. The breath passing through. The sound of waiting. |
| **V** | Vault, heaven | Humming. The resonance of the dome. The Terminal's voice. |
| **Z** | Zayin | Buzzing. Division. The cut. A blade drawn — or a word that severs. |
| **Y** | Yod | Reaching. The hand extended. A question that rises at the end. |

---

## Vowels

Telumë favors **open vowels** — sounds that fill the dome.

| Sound | Example | Feel |
|-------|---------|------|
| **E** (eh) | Telumë, Aleph | The most common vowel. Neutral, open, waiting. |
| **A** (ah) | Dalet, Taw | Wide. The mouth open. Awe or dread. |
| **U** (oo) | Telumë, vault | Deep. The sound of the dome itself. Resonance. |
| **I** (ee) | Sibyl, Zayin | High. Thin. The sound of something small and persistent. |
| **O** (oh) | Yod, Qoph | Round. Completion. The circle of the vault. |

---

## Stress and Rhythm

- Stress falls on the **first syllable** unless marked otherwise: **TEL**-umë, **DA**-let, **TAW**
- Words are short. One to three syllables. The old names are compact — they fit on stone.
- Compound names (Witch's Foot, Edge of the Vault) are translations from an older tongue. The original names are shorter. Harsher. Lost.

## The Rule of Names

1. **Old names are short.** One or two syllables. They fit on stone.
2. **New names are descriptive.** The Witch's Foot. The Edge of the Vault. The Terminal. These are translations — the old names are underneath, waiting.
3. **Glyphs are never pronounced in the text.** The reader sees 𐤃. The character may say "Dalet." But the narration does not phoneticize the glyph. The glyph is.
