---

**GLOSS — TABLET ONE:**

*Nu DINGIR.MEŠ-uš kuit ḫantezziš ēšta*
"Now — what was the first thing among the gods"
— Every Hittite legal document opens with *nu* — "now, and so, therefore." Not a word for present time but a logical connector. The text begins in the middle of a thought that started before writing.

*nu ēpir GIM-an ḫaršanalli*
"and they took it as one takes a skull-cup"
— *ḫaršanalli* — a ritual vessel made from the skull of a defeated enemy. The first thing the gods took was taken the way victors take trophies. The cosmological act is framed as conquest.

*nu-za MU.KAM.ḪI.A ḫinkān uizzi*
"and the years come toward death"
— *ḫinkān* — death, fate, the appointed end. Years don't pass in Hittite — they *come toward* you. Time is not something you move through. It arrives.

*nu memiyanniš kī ēšta*
"and the word — this — was"
— *memiyanni* — the word, the matter, the thing spoken. The Hittite word for language is also the word for legal matter. A word is always simultaneously a speech act and a legal fact.

*URU.ANTI-aš-ma-aš-kán anda*
"But as for the city that stood against —"
— *URU.ANTI* — the cuneiform compound for "the opposing city." The Tower. Named not by its builders' name but by its relationship to the gods. It is defined by its opposition, not its construction.

*URU-LIM kuin ḫalziškanzi*
"which they kept calling the great city"
— *ḫalziškanzi* — iterative verb form. They kept calling it, repeatedly, insistently. The name was an act of will repeated until it became true. The Tower was the great city because they said so, over and over, until the saying made it so.

*n-at DINGIR-LIM-ni pittiyatten*
"entrust it — do not — to the god"
— A legal formula inverted. Normally *pittiyatten* means "you (plural) shall entrust." Here the syntax breaks — the tablet is damaged or the scribe hesitated. What was not to be entrusted to the god? The city? The word? The covenant? The gap is the oldest editorial decision in the Haldorian archive.

*Nu LUGAL-uš kuitki mān*
"Now as for the king — if there were such a thing —"
— *kuitki* — "something, somewhat, if at all." The Hittite legal hedge. The king is introduced as a conditional. *If* there were a king. The Pankus reads this as the founding legal argument against monarchy — the king is grammatically hypothetical.

*LUGAL-uš lē ēšta*
"a king shall not be"
— *lē* — the Hittite prohibitive. Not "there is no king" but "a king shall not be." A legal prohibition, not a description of reality. The Pankus assembly has this line carved above every meeting hall entrance in every city-state except Tsarnigrad, where it was carved and then partially effaced and then recarved by a subsequent administration.

*PANKUŠ-aš-ma-kán anda*
"but the assembly — within —"
— *PANKUŠ* appearing for the first time in the document. "Within the assembly" — the assembly as container. You are inside the Pankus the way you are inside a building. It has walls. It has a threshold. The *-kán anda* construction always implies interior space.

*PANKUŠ pittiyatten ŠA DINGIR.MEŠ*
"entrust the assembly to the gods"
— The sentence that was broken in the previous tablet now completes itself. Don't entrust the city to the god. Entrust the *assembly* to the gods. The political body, not the physical city, is the sacred object.

**GLOSS — TABLET TWO:**

*Tiwad-ati ḫantawatin*
"The sun-god — in front of the lord"
— *Tiwad* — the Luwian sun deity, distinct from the Hittite sun goddess. Male solar deity. The shift from Hittite to Luwian is also a shift in solar theology — a different sun, a different gender, a different relationship to authority. The Sūrya scholars who have seen copies of this tablet find *Tiwad* deeply significant. The Sun-King's court has suppressed three separate translations.

*piran ḫuiššuwanza*
"living in front, alive before"
— *ḫuiššuwanza* — alive, living, the animate state. The sun god is alive *in front of* the lord — preceding authority, preceding law. The sun as precedent. What comes before the king comes before the law.

*Tiwad-ati uratin*
"The sun-god — behind the great one"
— The same sun god is now *behind* the great one. Two consecutive lines place the sun god both before and behind authority simultaneously. The Luwian theological position: the sun precedes and follows all power. Power exists inside the sun's brackets.

*arḫa ḫuiššuwanza*
"living away, alive apart"
— *arḫa* — away, apart, separated. The third position of the sun god: separated from authority entirely. Before, behind, and apart. Three positions that together describe a complete surround — the sun god encompasses authority without being identical to it.

*Ura pittiyanza*
"The great covenant"
— *ura* — great, large, the Luwian superlative for magnitude. *pittiyanza* — the covenanted thing, what has been entrusted. The great covenant appearing here for the first time in the Luwian tablet. Cognate with the Hittite *pittiya* but inflected differently — in Luwian the covenant is a *thing*, a noun, not a verbal action. It has already happened. It is not being made. It exists.

*pittiyanza ura*
"covenanted — great"
— The same two words reversed. This is the Luwian chiastic structure — mirror-line theology. The great covenant and the covenanted greatness are not the same thing but they rhyme. The Tihomir monks spent forty years arguing about whether this reversal was grammatical or theological.

*kī ḫandanza*
"this — arranged, set in order"
— *ḫandanza* — arranged, established, the past passive participle of *ḫand-* (to arrange, to put in order). What was arranged. The cosmic order as something that was *put* in order by an agent who is not named. Passive construction — the arranger is absent from the text.

*ḫandanza kī*
"arranged — this"
— Reversed again. The chiasm continues. The arrangement and the thing arranged are kept in perpetual mirror relation. Neither precedes the other. This is the Luwian answer to the question of which came first — the order or the thing ordered. The answer is: they face each other.

*Tarḫunt-ati wāti*
"The storm god — he went"
— *Tarḫunt* — the storm god, appearing now in the Luwian tablet after his Hittite appearance in Tablet One. *wāti* — he went, he departed. The storm god left. This is the theological crisis at the center of the Nešatta Codex — the storm god's departure. He was present at the Collapse and then he went. Where he went is not recorded.

*wāti Tarḫunt-ati*
"He went — the storm god"
— Reversed. The departure stated twice in mirror form. The chiasm applied to the most significant event in the tablet. The storm god's departure is so significant it must be said forwards and backwards, as if the reversal could undo it or at least fully account for it.

*mān ura pittiyai*
"if the great one covenants"
— *mān* — if, when, the Luwian conditional. Suddenly the covenant is conditional. If the great one covenants. The great covenant of the previous lines was stated as existing fact — and now it is placed in doubt by the conditional. Did the great covenant happen? The tablet seems uncertain.

*mān pittiyai ura*
"if the covenant is great"
— The condition reversed. Not just whether the covenanting happened but whether the covenant, if it happened, was great. Two separate doubts in mirror form. The Luwian theological method applied to its own foundational claim.

---

**GLOSS — TABLET THREE:**

*Nu-za kī memiyanniš*
"Now — this word/matter"
— The *-za* particle indicates reflexive action — the text turning back on itself. "Now this matter — for itself." The legal document becoming aware of its own existence as a legal document.

*kī pittiyaš*
"this covenant"
— *kī* — this, the proximal demonstrative. Not "that covenant" (distant, historical) but "this covenant" (present, immediate). The text insists on its own immediacy regardless of when it is read. Every time the Pankus assembly reads this line they are reading "this covenant" — the one in their hands, the one being recited, the one that is happening now.

*kī URU.ANTI-aš*
"this city-that-stood-against"
— The Tower named again. But now with *kī* — this, proximate. The Tower is not historical distance. It is *this* — present, immediate, here. The Nešatta Stone's theological claim: the Collapse is not past. It is continuously present. The Tower is always falling.

*lē šarrater*
"shall not be broken"
— *šarrater* — the breaking of an oath, a treaty violation, the fracturing of a legal agreement. The most serious legal prohibition in Hittite law. Applied here to the covenant itself — the covenant against breaking covenants. The meta-legal layer. You cannot break the rule against breaking rules.

*URU-aš-ma-kán anda*
"as for the city — within"
"as for the covenant — within"
"as for the word — within"
— Three parallel constructions. City, covenant, word — all with *anda* (within, inside). All three are containers. You live inside the city. You live inside the covenant. You live inside the word. The Hittite legal imagination places the human being perpetually interior to these structures.

*n-at-kán lē arḫa*
"and they shall not be taken away"
— *arḫa* — away, apart, the separation. What is inside shall not be removed from inside. The city, covenant, and word shall not be separated from each other. This is the Nešatta Codex's central legal claim — the three are bound together and the binding is permanent.

*Nu kī PANKUŠ-aš*
"Now — this assembly"
*nu kī pittiyaš*
"Now — this covenant"
*nu kī ḫalkiš*
"Now — this grain / this foundation"
— The triple *nu kī* construction. Three times "now this." The assembly, the covenant, and *ḫalkiš* — the word that gives the document its name. *Ḫalki* means grain. It also means foundation. The Hittite word for bread-grain and the word for the base of a structure are the same word. What feeds you and what holds you up are linguistically identical. The Pankus is the grain. The Pankus is the foundation.

*n-at ŪL kappuuanzi*
"and these are not counted"
— *kappuuanzi* — to count, to enumerate, to assess. The assembly, the covenant, the foundation — they are not countable. You cannot measure them. The Luwian chiasm said the covenant existed as fact; the Hittite legal layer now says it exists beyond measurement. Outside the accounting system entirely.

*GIM-an-ma-kán*
"But just as —"
— The comparative conjunction. The text pivoting to analogy. Just as something — the analogy is coming.

*MU.KAM.ḪI.A ḫinkān uizzi*
"the years come toward death"
— The same line from Tablet One. The years coming toward death. The repetition is not accident — the text is building a structure. Just as the years come toward death —

*n-at-kán PANKUŠ-ni anda*
"so within the assembly"
— The analogy completes. Just as years come toward death, so within the assembly — the next line will complete the thought but the tablet breaks here. Bogumil's note: *"The clay is damaged. I have left the gap. The gap is not mine."*

*Nu-za lē LUGAL-uš*
"Now — there shall not be a king"
*nu-za lē LUGAL.GAL-uš*
"Now — there shall not be a great king"
— The prohibition stated twice, escalating. Not just no king but no *great* king. The Pankus legal tradition distinguishes between these — a *lugal* (king) and a *lugal.gal* (great king, high king, king of kings). Both prohibited. The second prohibition implies someone proposed one.

*PANKUŠ-aš-ma-za*
"but the assembly — for itself"
— *-za* again — the reflexive. The assembly for itself. Self-governing, self-referential, self-contained. The legal subject that is also its own object.

*pittiyatten ŠA KUR*
"you shall entrust to the land"
— *ŠA KUR* — of the land, belonging to the land. The assembly shall be entrusted to the land itself. Not to a king, not to a god, not to a founding document. To the land. The Bay of Ealdforn as the sovereign. The water as the authority. The Kichigamswak confederacy, when they eventually heard this line translated, said: *"Yes. This is what we have always known."*

*Namma-ma-kán*
"Furthermore —"
— The legal addendum marker. What follows is supplementary but binding.

*ḫalkiuš pittiyaš*
"the covenant of the grain/foundation"
— The title of the document appearing inside the document. The covenant names itself. Self-referential legal structure — the Nešatta Codex covenanting its own existence.

*ḫalziššanzi memiyanniš*
"they shall keep calling the word"
— *ḫalziššanzi* — the iterative again, the same verb used for the Tower's builders who kept calling it the great city. Now used for the word itself — keep calling it, keep speaking it, repeat it until the repetition makes it true. The Pankus assembly reads the Nešatta Codex aloud at every meeting. This line is why. The reading is the covenanting. The speaking is the legal act.

*n-at lē šarrater*
"and it shall not be broken"
— The closing legal seal. The same prohibition from the opening of Tablet Three. The document closes the same way it opened. The covenant against breaking the covenant, stated at the beginning and the end, with everything in between held inside it like grain inside a jar, like people inside a city, like words inside a mouth that keeps speaking.

## COLOPHON
*In the hand of Bogumil, Tihomir monastery, Year 847:*

"Seven tablets. I have copied three. The fourth is missing — removed before my time, the abbot says, by a delegation from Tsarnigrad who came in the night and left before morning. The fifth is in a script I do not recognize — not Hittite, not Luwian, not any hand I have been trained to read. The sixth is intact but sealed with clay impressed with a glyph I will not reproduce here. The seventh tablet is blank. The clay was fired. The surface was prepared. Nothing was written on it. I have copied the blank tablet as a blank page. The blank page is here. You are reading the blank tablet now. I do not know what this means. The abbot says it means the covenant is not finished. The storm god has not returned to write the final tablet. We are waiting. The Pankus has been waiting since before our order existed. The fire holds."
