The Comitia Publia

Three Hundred Citizens · Chamber of Voices
Forum · Caepton
The Assembly of the People. Three hundred citizens chosen annually by lot from the census rolls. The Comitia initiates legislation, controls taxation and expenditure, hears petitions, and declares emergencies. The Tribune of the People is elected from among its members.
Any citizen who has completed their Sky-Vigil is eligible. One year of nightly sky observation, recorded and submitted to the Abbey. Refusing service is permitted — but the refuser's name is recorded in the Charon Archive as one who turned away. This is not a crime. It is remembered.
Current Tribune: The Harvester. Former shrine-keeper from Virgo. Elected by the Comitia. Three months remaining in her term. Has threatened to veto the war budget unless the Senate funds the Leo shrine.
The Forbidden Laws

Three things the Republic's constitution explicitly forbids. Not because they are unwise, but because they have been tried — in earlier republics, in earlier wars — and each time, the result was dissolution.

  1. No Mortal May Claim a Sign
    The signs belong to the gods. Mortals may build shrines, burn tokens, and appeal for Olympian return — but they cannot claim territory in the war. To do so invites the attention of both factions simultaneously. No Republic has survived that.
  2. No Office May Be Held for Life
    The Tribune serves one year. The Comitia is redrawn annually. Senators serve at the pleasure of their appointing institutions. Justices must be reconfirmed every seven years. The war breeds the delusion of indispensability. The constitution starves it.
  3. The Empty Throne Must Remain Empty
    Athena's seat in the Hall of Seven Thrones cannot be filled — not by a mortal, not by a vote, not by a decree. The throne is empty because Libra is empty. If Athena returns, she may sit. Until then, the emptiness is the Republic's conscience.

RATIFIED IN THE 347TH YEAR OF THE WAR

The founders — twelve astrologers, six Sky-Watchers and six Void-Seers, deadlocked until the Balancer among them proposed the third way — signed the Charter in the Hall of Seven Thrones, beneath the empty seat. Their names are carved into the Censor's throne, wrapping around the stone, continuing onto the floor.
