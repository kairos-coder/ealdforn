The Constitutional Prohibitions
The Forbidden Laws

Three things the Republic's constitution explicitly forbids. Each has been tried — in earlier republics, in earlier wars — and each time, the result was dissolution.

  1. No Mortal May Claim a Sign
    The signs belong to the gods. Mortals may build shrines and appeal for Olympian return — but they cannot claim territory in the war. To do so invites the attention of both factions simultaneously. No Republic has survived that.
  2. No Office May Be Held for Life
    The Tribune serves one year. The Comitia is redrawn annually. Justices must be reconfirmed every seven years. The war breeds the delusion of indispensability. The constitution starves it.
  3. The Empty Throne Must Remain Empty
    Athena's seat in the Hall of Seven Thrones cannot be filled — not by a mortal, not by a vote, not by a decree. The throne is empty because Libra is empty. If Athena returns, she may sit. Until then, the emptiness is the Republic's conscience.
