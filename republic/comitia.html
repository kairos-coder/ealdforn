Eligibility & Sortition

The Sky-Vigil. Every citizen who wishes to stand for the Comitia must complete one year of nightly sky observation — recording planetary positions, moon phases, and any omens witnessed. The completed vigil is submitted to the Abbey for the Censor's review. Upon acceptance, the citizen's name is entered into the census rolls.

The Sortition. On the winter solstice of each year, 300 names are drawn publicly in the Forum of Caepton under the light of the full or waxing moon. The draw is conducted by the Censor Maximus and witnessed by the Tribune of the People. No citizen may serve consecutive terms. No Senator or Justice may serve in the Comitia while holding office — though former officeholders are eligible.

Refusal. A citizen whose name is drawn may refuse service. The refusal is recorded in the Charon Archive as "one who turned away from the Republic's call." This is not a crime. It is not a punishment. It is simply remembered.

About the Comitia Publia

The Comitia Publia is the voice of the people. It is the only body that can initiate legislation, levy taxes, and declare emergencies. The Senate may refuse. The Court may review. The Tribune may veto. But the Comitia speaks first.

The Assembly meets in the Forum of Caepton, in the open air, under the sky. No session may be held indoors. No session may be held under a solar eclipse. The people must be able to see the heavens they are governed by.
