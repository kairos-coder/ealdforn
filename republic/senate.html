The Olympian Families — Blood-Right
♊
House Geminian
Seat of Gemini · Element: Air
503 years — the oldest continuous seat in the Senate
Current Senator
Lucia Geminiana
41 years · Speaks with two voices — a childhood fever left her with a permanent double-tone. The family calls it a sign: she speaks for both twins.
Status
Olympian · Apollo and Hephaestus both walk Gemini. Two gods in one sign. The Geminiani have produced more Censors than any other family. The Abbey's transcription halls are effectively a Geminian institution.
🩸
Selection Method
Blood-Right — Inherited by Dynasty
The Geminiani have never faced sortition. Their seat is the oldest because Gemini has never fallen. "The first draft is for the gods. The second is for history."
Senate Role: The Geminiani are the Senate's diplomats and scholars. When the chamber divides, Lucia Geminiana speaks last among the Olympian families — not because she commands, but because she synthesizes. Her double-voice carries weight. The other families listen for both tones.

House Aretias
Seat of Aries · Element: Fire
412 years · Ancestral Seat: Glimthaven
Current Senator
Mars Aretias
Former War Council commander. Right arm crushed at the Siege of the Tides. Signs all documents with his left hand — a mark of pride.
Status
Olympian · Hera and Poseidon both walk Aries. The sign is held but crowded — two gods with different agendas. The Aretii watch for signs of departure.
🩸
Selection Method
Blood-Right — Inherited by Dynasty
Every Aretian Senator has served in the War Council before taking the seat. The ram's horn is blown only when Aries is threatened. It has been blown twice.
Senate Role: The Senate's hawks. The Aretii always urge reinforcement of contested signs, always warn that the Underworld's next push is imminent. They are terrified of Aries falling — not for the loss of the seat, but because Aries is a fire sign. If it falls, Phlegethon rises.

House Taurinus
Seat of Taurus · Element: Earth
287 years · Ancestral Seat: The Granary District, Caepton
Current Senator
Porcia Taurina
67 years. Has never spoken a word in the Senate chamber. Communicates through written notes. Her most recent note: "Feed the soldiers or they will eat the citizens. Fund the granaries."
Status
Olympian · Ares alone walks Taurus. One god, one sign, one family. The Taurini hold the Aedile's office more often than any other house — three times in the Republic's history.
🩸
Selection Method
Blood-Right — Inherited by Dynasty
The Taurini bring a bronze bull to every session. It has never been moved by anyone outside the family. Every Taurinus Senator has died in office — from age, not violence. They regard this as pride.
Senate Role: The Senate's pragmatists. When debate runs long, Porcia Taurina's notes arrive — short, blunt, and impossible to argue with. The family that feeds the Republic does not need to speak loudly.

House Cancri
Seat of Cancer · Element: Water · STELLIUM
198 years · Ancestral Seat: Tidal island in the Bay
Current Senator
Selene Cancra
33 years — the youngest Senator. Inherited the seat four years ago when her mother drowned swimming at midnight under a full moon. The body was never recovered.
Status
Stellium · Three gods walk Cancer — Hermes, Aphrodite, and Zeus. This is the strongest Olympian concentration in the sky. But Hermes will leave by June 23. The stellium is temporary.
🩸
Selection Method
Blood-Right — Inherited by Dynasty
The Cancri wear a ceremonial crab-shell helmet during private rites. The shell is replaced each generation. Selene wears her mother's shell. She does not speak of the drowning.
Senate Role: The Senate's emotional center. Selene Cancra speaks last in every debate — not by rule, but by tradition. She names what everyone else is feeling. The Cancri fear the stellium's dissolution. If any god leaves, Cancer becomes merely Olympian — and the Cancri have no experience with merely.
The Underworld Families — Sortition & Appointment
♌
House Leonid
Seat of Leo · Element: Fire · Underworld
340 years · Won sortition this year
Current Senator
Aurelian Leonid
Firstborn son. Born without a roar — literally silent at birth, crying only when the midwife spoke his name. The Leonid curse since Leo fell.
Status
Underworld · Leo has been empty since Apollo abandoned the Lion. The Leonids have won sortition every year since — but each year the margin narrows. The Leo shrines are burning double offerings awaiting the solstice.
🎲
Selection Method
Sortition — Drawn by Lot with Shrine-Keepers
If Apollo enters Leo at the solstice, the sortition ends. The Leonids reclaim blood-right. If Apollo turns away, the lots continue — and next year, a shrine-keeper may take the seat.
Senate Role: The Leonids are the face of what every Olympian family fears. They sit in the chamber by luck, not by right. Their votes are watched. Their alliances are scrutinized. Aurelian Leonid has not spoken in a Senate debate in two years — not because he cannot, but because he will not give the other families the satisfaction of hearing his voice tremble.

Seat of Virgo · Element: Earth · Underworld
265 years · Won sortition this year
Current Senator
Demetria Virgina
The Harvester's cousin. Former shrine-keeper herself. Won sortition by a single lot — the closest margin in Virgina history.
Status
Underworld · Demeter abandoned Virgo. The Virginii have held the seat through sortition for three generations. The family's ancestral granaries are still the largest in Caepton — but the Aedile's office has passed to the Taurini twice since Virgo fell.
🎲
Selection Method
Sortition — Drawn by Lot with Shrine-Keepers
The Virginii are the only Underworld family that actively funds their sign's shrines — not out of piety, but out of strategy. More shrine-keepers means more names in the lottery. The Virginii want the odds diluted.
Senate Role: The Virginii vote with the Olympian families on agricultural matters and with the Underworld families on everything else. They are the Senate's swing vote on harvest legislation — and the Aedile Maximus, Marcus Granarius, is not a Virgina. This is a source of quiet, permanent tension.

The Contested Family — Council-Appointed

House Aquilae
Seat of Aquarius · Element: Air · CONTESTED
156 years · Appointed by the Council of Elders
Current Senator
Nestor Aquilae
Balancer. Appointed by the Council of Elders three years ago. Has not spoken in session for the past three meetings. Holds the tiebreaker. Has refused to break three successive ties.
Status
Contested · Artemis (Moon) and Hades (Pluto) both walk Aquarius. The only sign where both factions are physically present. The war is not coming to Aquarius. It is already there.
📜
Selection Method
Appointed by the Council of Elders
The Aquarian Senator is never elected, never drawn by lot, never inherited. The Council of Elders chooses — because the contested sign requires a Senator who can see both sides of the war.
Senate Role: The tiebreaker. When the Senate divides 6-5, Nestor Aquilae decides. He has refused to decide on three war measures — funding for the Leo shrines, reinforcement of the Aquarius contested zone, and the declaration of a State of Celestial Emergency. His silence is not indecision. It is strategy. "The contested sign must remain neutral while the threshold shifts." The Senate does not know what that means. Nestor Aquilae has not explained.
The Tiebreaker
With twelve Senators and a 7-5 sky, the Senate is perpetually one vote from deadlock. The Aquarian Senator — alone among the twelve — casts the tiebreaking vote. Nestor Aquilae has refused to break three successive ties, citing the Augur's dark moon advisory and the contested status of his sign. The Senate cannot force him to vote. The Constitution does not require a Senator to speak. It only requires them to sit.
About the Senatus Ealdfornicus

The Senate was established in the 347th Year of the War, alongside the Libraen Court and the Comitia Publia. Twelve Senators — one per zodiac sign — sit in the Senate Chamber at Meridian, the city built on the Republic's equator.

Senators are chosen by the sign they represent. Olympian-held signs pass by blood-right — dynasty and inheritance. Underworld-held signs are subject to sortition — the family must submit their name to the lottery alongside the shrine-keepers of their sign. Contested signs are appointed by the Council of Elders. Sagittarius and Capricorn are appointed by the Abbey and the Registry respectively — the only Underworld signs exempt from sortition, because memory and oaths cannot be left to chance.

The Senate ratifies legislation, declares war and peace, approves treaties with chthonic forces, and confirms the Republic's Telos. It cannot initiate legislation — that power belongs to the Comitia. It cannot judge — that power belongs to the Libraen Court. It cannot tax — that power belongs to the Assembly. The Senate's power is in its refusal. A bill that passes the Comitia but fails the Senate is dead.
