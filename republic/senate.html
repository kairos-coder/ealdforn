Chaos
The primordial force that existed before the gods. When all signs of an element fall void, Chaos speaks. It does not threaten — it simply is, and always was. Chaos dissolves boundaries. It unmakes the agreements that hold the Republic together. The river Lethe rises when Chaos stirs.
💀
Decay
The slow entropy of all institutions. Decay is not dramatic — it is the forgotten shrine, the unread archive, the oath repeated until it loses meaning. Thanatos is its general. The Erinyes are its enforcers. Decay wins not by battle but by erosion. Every year a sign remains Underworld-held, Decay advances.

⬡ The Coherence Covenant ⬡

We, the Six Blessed Houses of the Senatus Ealdfornicus, holding the Olympian thrones by generative right and divine manifestation, do hereby pledge:

To cohere. Not to merge — each house retains its sovereignty, its sign, its god. But to act as one when the Republic faces dissolution. A divided Senate is Decay's victory. A united Senate is civilization's answer.

To fight Chaos not with force but with form. Chaos is the primordial — it cannot be defeated, only bounded. We bind it with law, with record, with memory, with the Ephemeris Publica that reads the sky before it strikes.

To resist Decay not with panic but with practice. Decay wins by erosion. We answer with renewal — shrine-funding for the Underworld-held signs, upkeep of the archives, the Tribune's veto ready against any measure that would weaken the Republic's foundations.

To honor the Unblessed Houses. They held these seats through centuries of war before we arrived. We do not replace them — we join them. Their counsel is the memory of the Republic. Our generation is the Republic's future. Both are necessary.

To keep the Empty Throne empty. Athena's seat in the Hall of Seven Thrones shall remain unfilled. Not by mortal, not by vote, not by AI decree. The emptiness is the Republic's conscience. When Athena returns to Libra, she may sit. Until then, we govern in her absence.
